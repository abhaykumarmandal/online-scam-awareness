STOP

Pause before acting

Urgency is a common warning sign. Do not rush to click a link, pay money or share personal information.

CHECK

Verify independently

Check the sender, website address, company, landlord or official source using contact details you found yourself.

REPORT

Report suspicious messages to your university, bank, platform or another appropriate official service.

Common scams affecting students

Phishing emails and scam texts

Messages may imitate a university, bank, delivery company or student-finance service and ask you to click a link or sign in.

Fake jobs and money-mule offers

Be cautious of jobs promising easy money, asking for upfront fees or requesting use of your bank account to receive or transfer funds.

Accommodation and deposit scams

Do not pay a deposit before verifying the property, landlord or agency and checking the documents and payment method.

Banking, QR and payment scams

Do not share passwords, PINs or one-time passcodes. Avoid scanning unknown QR codes or paying through links sent in urgent messages.

Student scam-safety checklist

Before clicking

  • Check the full sender address and link destination.
  • Look for pressure, urgency, threats or unrealistic offers.
  • Open the official website or app separately.

Before paying or sharing

  • Verify the organisation, job, property or person independently.
  • Never share passwords, PINs or OTP codes.
  • Ask a trusted person or official support service for help.
Remember: If something feels urgent, secret, unusual or too good to be true, stop and verify it before acting.
